# DMCA Takedown Policy

This repository takes intellectual property rights seriously. We will respond promptly and in good faith to all valid DMCA takedown notices.

---

## Submitting a Takedown Request

If you are a copyright holder (or authorized agent) and believe that content in this repository infringes your copyright, please submit a notice containing **all of the following**:

1. **Your identity** — Your full legal name, title (if acting as an agent), company name, and contact information (email, phone, mailing address).

2. **Identification of the copyrighted work** — Describe the original copyrighted work you claim has been infringed (e.g., title, revision number, publication date, ISBN/part number if applicable).

3. **Identification of the infringing material** — Provide the **exact URL(s)** or file path(s) within this repository of the content you claim is infringing.

4. **Good faith statement** — A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

5. **Accuracy statement** — A statement that the information in your notice is accurate, and **under penalty of perjury**, that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

6. **Signature** — Your physical or electronic signature.

## Our Response Process

Upon receipt of a valid notice:

1. We will acknowledge receipt within **48 hours**
2. The identified content will be **removed or disabled within 7 days** (usually faster)
3. We will notify the person who contributed the content, if identifiable
4. We will file a record of the takedown

---

## Counter-Notices

If you believe content was removed in error, you may submit a counter-notice under 17 U.S.C. § 512(g). A valid counter-notice must include:

1. Your contact information
2. Identification of the removed material and its previous location
3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake or misidentification
4. Your consent to jurisdiction of the federal court in your district
5. Your physical or electronic signature

---

## Repeat Infringers

This repository maintains a policy of removing repeat infringers in accordance with GitHub's Terms of Service and the DMCA.

---

## Public Domain & Regulatory Documents

Documents originating from the following sources are generally considered public domain in their respective jurisdictions and are not subject to DMCA:

- **FAA** — Federal Aviation Administration (U.S. Government works, 17 U.S.C. § 105)
- **ICAO** — Some documents; verify per document
- **EASA** — Most official regulatory texts (AMC, CS) are freely available; verify per document
- **National AIPs** — Varies by country

If you believe a document marked as public domain is actually under copyright, please contact us with documentation.
