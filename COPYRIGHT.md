## Regulatory & Government Documents

Documents produced by government agencies may be in the **public domain** depending on jurisdiction:

- **United States (FAA):** Works of the U.S. federal government are not subject to U.S. copyright under 17 U.S.C. § 105. FAA Advisory Circulars, Orders, and regulations are generally free to reproduce.
- **EASA:** Most regulatory texts (Certification Specifications, AMC, GM) are freely available from the EASA website and may be reproduced with attribution.
- **ICAO:** ICAO documents are **not** public domain; ICAO retains copyright. Only freely-available excerpts or documents explicitly released should be hosted.
- **National AIPs / Charts:** Copyright status varies by country. Many national AIPs are copyrighted.

When in doubt, a document is assumed to be **under copyright** unless there is clear documentation otherwise.
